Types of Solar Systems

Choose the Right Solar Setup

On-Grid Solar System (Grid-Tied)

Connected to the electricity grid; excess solar can be exported, often earning credits via net metering.

  • Lowest upfront cost; use grid as virtual battery.
  • Earn credits for exported energy (where available).
  • Does not run during outages unless paired with batteries and backup circuitry.

Off-Grid Solar System

Standalone system using batteries to store energy, ideal for remote sites without grid access.

  • Total independence from the grid.
  • Requires correctly sized battery & inverter for 24/7 supply.
  • Design & maintenance planning are critical for reliability.

Hybrid Solar System

Combines batteries with a grid connection, store energy for backup while importing/exporting as needed.

  • Works during outages using battery backup.
  • Optimize self-consumption with time-of-use strategies.
  • Best balance of independence, reliability, and ROI.

Types of Solar Mounting Structures

Select the Ideal Mounting Solution

Ground-Mounted Solar Systems

Designed for large-scale installations ranging from 10 MW to 200 MW, these systems are installed on open land to maximize solar energy generation. They are ideal for utility-scale projects and grid integration.

  • Optimized land use for maximum yield on utility-scale sites.
  • Scalable from 10 MW up to 200 MW and beyond.
  • Well-suited for grid connection and centralized O&M.

Rooftop Solar Systems

Suitable for residential, commercial, and industrial buildings, rooftop systems utilize available roof space to generate clean energy efficiently. They help reduce electricity costs and support sustainability goals.

  • Leverages existing roof space, no additional land required.
  • Cuts electricity bills for homes, businesses, and factories.
  • Accelerates corporate and household sustainability targets.
